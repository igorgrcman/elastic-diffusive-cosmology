\paragraph{Constraint status.}
The Fermi constant $G_F$ is currently a \textbf{constraint}, not a derivation.
In generic 5D localized-fermion setups, naive overlap integrals give $g_5^2 I_4 \sim O(1)$
because normalized wavefunctions satisfy $\int |f|^2 = 1$, forcing $\int |f|^4 \sim 1/\text{width}$
to be naturally large.
To reproduce the tiny observed $G_F \approx 10^{-5}$ GeV$^{-2}$, EDC must supply
\textbf{either} (a) a heavy mediator at the electroweak scale $M_{\text{eff}} \sim 100$ GeV,
\textbf{or} (b) strong overlap suppression via chirality separation---the mechanism
of Projection-Reduction Lemma Case (B), where $\varepsilon = \int w_L w_R \ll 1$
(see \texttt{edc\_papers/\_shared/lemmas/projection\_reduction\_lemma.tex}).
Therefore, the BVP overlap calculation is a \textbf{decisive falsification channel}:
if neither mechanism delivers the required suppression, the mode-overlap picture fails.
