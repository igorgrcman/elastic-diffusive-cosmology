\section{Problem Statement}
% ==============================================================================

\begin{problem}[$G_F$ from Geometry]
Derive the Fermi constant $G_F \approx 1.166 \times 10^{-5}$ GeV$^{-2}$ from
5D thick-brane geometry, without using $M_W$ or $g_{\text{weak}}$ as inputs.
\end{problem}

\subsection{Why This Matters}

In the Standard Model, $G_F$ is related to $W$-boson exchange:
\begin{equation}
G_F = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{8} \frac{g^2}{M_W^2}
\end{equation}
But $g$ and $M_W$ are themselves parameters---not explained.

EDC claims weak interactions emerge from thick-brane dynamics. The effective
coupling $G_F$ should therefore be calculable from:
\begin{itemize}
  \item The 5D mediator mass scale
  \item Mode profile overlaps in the thick brane
  \item Geometric suppression factors
\end{itemize}

\begin{equation}

\begin{equation}

\begin{equation}
